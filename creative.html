Creating A Great Lawn

There is a lot to know in creating a healthy turf.
Last year, I decided to try and work on my own lawn instead of hiring it out.
Here, you will find how I went about renovating it.


There are a few needed ingredients in order to grow a healthy lawn:

  • Roundup (only if you are doing a full renovation)
  • Starter Fertilizer
  • Quality Grass Seed
  • Lime (if soil ph is low)
  • Pre-emergent to keep weeds out
  • Frequent Watering
  • Aeration

*It is very important to have a soil test done before you begin seeding
Most of the yards in TN will have a clay soil. In order to loosen it properly, you must use an aerator.
I killed about 90% of the lawn off with Roundup because it was in bad shape with a lot of Poa Annua and Trivialis.
Sometimes, it is just easier to start over.

Below is what the lawn looked like after killing it off with roundup.


Compost can used to cover the seed in order to help hold moisture (as seen below).
It is very important to keep the see moist until full germination. That means watering at least 3 times a day.
